Credit Card Arbitrage

Basic concept: Use someone with good credit ‚Üí get cashback ‚Üí pay immediately ‚Üí they owe you = free money from rewards (1-5%).

Reality check: Routing purchases through someone else's card for cashback splitting is mathematically meh. On $500/month you'd earn $5-25 to split. The coordination overhead (tracking every purchase, sending cash back) isn't worth it ‚Äî especially the mental load.

When it works: Only worth it on high-category spending you'd do anyway (e.g. 5% groceries ‚Äî hand them cash for the grocery run). Don't bother routing all spending through them.

The Real Play: Authorized User

Way more valuable than cashback splitting. This is credit building, not pocket change.

  • Their card's full payment history gets added to your credit report
  • If the card has years of on-time payments, your score can jump 30-80 points
  • You don't need to hold or use the physical card
  • They keep full control ‚Äî can remove you with one phone call
  • Zero risk to them ‚Äî their score isn't affected

The Pitch: "You don't give me a card. You don't give me access. Nothing changes for you. You just call the bank and add my name. That's it. It helps me build credit and costs you nothing."

Verified Money-Making Strategies

1. Bank Account Bonuses

Low Risk

Open checking accounts with signup bonuses ($200-500). Meet minimum requirements, close after 6 months, repeat.

Risk: Can affect ChexSystems score if done too frequently

2. Cashback Stacking

Low Risk

Use multiple cashback platforms on same purchase. Example: Rakuten (5%) + credit card (1.5%) = 6.5% total.

Platforms: Rakuten, Honey, TopCashback

3. Credit Card Churning

Medium Risk

Open cards for signup bonuses (50k-100k points). Meet minimum spend, use points, cancel after one year.

Warning: Can temporarily hurt credit score, requires discipline

4. Discounted Gift Cards

Low Risk

Buy gift cards at 10-20% off face value, use for regular purchases.

Platforms: Raise, CardCash, Gift Card Granny

5. Extended Warranty

Low Risk

Many cards double manufacturer warranty automatically. Free protection on electronics and appliances.

Can save $50-200+ on extended warranty purchases

6. Manufactured Spending

High Risk

Buy money orders with credit card ‚Üí deposit ‚Üí pay off card. Generates cashback/points with no net spend.

Warning: Banks crack down on this, high risk of account closure

7. Retail Arbitrage

Medium Risk

Buy clearance items ‚Üí resell on Amazon/eBay. Requires time, storage, shipping.

Can generate $500-2000/mo part-time

8. 0% APR Balance Transfer

Low Risk

Transfer high-interest debt to 0% APR card (12-21 months), pay off during promotional period.

Watch out for: Balance transfer fee (3-5%)

Credit Building (Start Here First)

If you have poor/no credit, focus here before churning:

1. Secured Credit Card

  • Put $200-500 deposit
  • Get equal credit limit
  • Use for small purchases, pay in full
  • After 6-12 months, upgrade to unsecured

2. Authorized User on Good Account

  • Get added to parent/spouse card with good history
  • Their full payment history reports to your credit
  • Instant boost to credit age/score (+30-80 points)
  • You don't need the physical card ‚Äî they keep full control
  • Zero risk to them ‚Äî one phone call to remove you anytime

3. Credit Builder Loan

  • Pay into savings account, build credit
  • Get money back after 12 months
  • Improves payment history

4. Rent/Utility Reporting

  • Services like Experian Boost add rent/utilities to credit report
  • Usually free or low cost
